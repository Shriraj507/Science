India’s Contribution to Satellite Technology Through GSAT and NAVIC

India’s Contribution to Satellite Technology

India has made significant strides in satellite technology, leveraging its advancements to address critical societal needs such as weather prediction, navigation, and disaster management. Two hallmark systems in this journey are GSAT (Geostationary Satellites) and NAVIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation). These programs underscore India's ability to harness space technology for both national and regional development.

GSAT: Geostationary Satellites

The GSAT series is a group of advanced communication satellites developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). These satellites provide invaluable services in weather monitoring, disaster management, and remote connectivity.

NAVIC: Navigation with Indian Constellation

NAVIC is India’s independent regional navigation satellite system, operational since 2018. It provides precise positioning, navigation, and timing services over India and neighboring regions up to 1,500 km beyond its borders.

Impact Across India

India’s advancements through GSAT and NAVIC exemplify its commitment to leveraging space technology for societal benefits. These programs not only enhance national resilience but also position India as a global leader in space science and satellite technology.
